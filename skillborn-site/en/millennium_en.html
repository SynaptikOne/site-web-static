Millennium Program

SynaptikOne is committed not only to presenting but also to providing solutions to the 7 Millennium Problems defined by the Clay Mathematics Institute. We develop innovative approaches in artificial intelligence and mathematics to tackle these 21st-century challenges.

Our Commitment to the 7 Problems

1. P ≠ NP?

SynaptikOne explores computational and AI methods to clarify the boundary between verifiable and solvable problems. Goal: move towards a proof and strengthen global cryptography security.

2. Riemann Hypothesis

We develop numerical tools to analyze the zeros of the zeta function and approach an experimental resolution, with applications in number theory and cybersecurity.

3. Navier–Stokes Equations

Using numerical simulation and deep learning, we study fluid turbulence and stability to contribute to the existence of global solutions.

4. Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture

SynaptikOne studies elliptic curves and post-quantum cryptography to demystify hidden properties of this conjecture and strengthen future security systems.

5. Hodge Conjecture

Our research combines geometry, topology, and neural networks to develop new approaches and approach a resolution of this key conjecture in modern mathematics.

6. Poincaré Conjecture (solved ✅)

Solved by Grigori Perelman in 2002 (officially validated in 2003). Our role: popularize this breakthrough, integrate it into educational tools, and inspire future generations of researchers.

7. Yang–Mills Theory and Mass Gap

SynaptikOne develops mathematical and physical models to make a novel contribution to this fundamental question in modern physics, essential for understanding elementary particles.
