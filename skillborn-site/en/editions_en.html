Edition & Access

The GENOME_COMPRESSOR project is developed in a spirit of scientific openness, ethical innovation, and exploration at the intersection of biology, mathematics, and technology.

Free Edition (Open source)

This edition is released under the MIT License and publicly available on GitHub. It represents the core of the project: a reliable and reproducible scientific base, designed to be understood, studied, modified , and shared.

  • Language: Python
  • Access : Free and open
  • License : MIT
  • Access the GitHub repository →

SECURE EDITION (Restricted Access)

An experimental extension of GENOME_COMPRESSOR, this version includes encryption modules (classical and bio-inspired) for sensitive applications in bioinformatics and data security. It is currently under advanced research.

  • Language : Rust (and Python)
  • Status : Under private development
  • Access : By request (Restricted license or academic/industrial collaboration)

To participate or request access:
contactsynaptikone@gmail.com

Access is reserved for recognized institutions (universities, R&D centers, public agencies, NGOs, etc.).
Each request is manually reviewed to ensure responsible and ethical use.

POST-QUANTUM EDITION (Restricted Access)

An advanced version of SECURE EDITION integrating post-quantum encryption algorithms, designed to meet future security challenges in the era of quantum computing. Intended for institutions, labs, or organization handling critical data.

  • Language: Rust
  • Status : Advanced prototype
  • Access : By request only, with a specific agreement

For access requests, partnership inquiries, or confidential collaboration:
contactsynaptikone@gmail.com

Access is strictly reserved for verified institutions. All requests are manually reviewed to ensure responsible usage.

Scientific & Community Access

SynaptikOne aims to create an open yet controlled research platform for sensitive modules. If you're a researcher, student, engineer, or enthusiast, you can request access, propose collaboration, or contribute to upcoming publications.

Specific licenses may be granted to eligible labs, universities, or open-source partners.

Transparency & Commitment

We believe science should remain free, reproducible, and ethical. No algorithm developed within this project will be commercialized without ensuring public access to its scientific foundation.
